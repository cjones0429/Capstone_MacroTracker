Here's some basic information on how you can calculate the amount of calories and/or macronutrients you need per day.


Calculate basal metabolic rate (BMR), or the calories your body burns simply by being alive.
For men: 10 x weight (kg) + 6.25 x height (cm) – 5 x age (y) + 5 (kcal / day)
For women: 10 x weight (kg) + 6.25 x height (cm) – 5 x age (y) -161 (kcal / day)

1 kg = 2.2 lbs | 1 inch = 2.54cm | 1 foot = 12in | y = years | kcal = calories

Then, this BMR count is multiplied, depending on your activity level:
Sedentary = 1.2
Lightly active = 1.375
Moderately active = 1.550
Very active = 1.725
Extra active = 1.9

The calorie count is then adjusted based on your goal:
Weight loss: Reduce by 10-20%
Weight gain: Add 500 calories
Weight maintenance: Unchanged

This calorie count is split into macronutrient percentages in the following ratios, based on splits commonly recommended by certified nutrition experts for muscle gain, weight loss, and weight maintenance.
Weight loss: 40 carbs/40 protein/20 fats
Weight gain: 40 carbs/30 protein/30 fats
Weight maintenance: 40 carbs/30 protein/30 fats

These daily grams of each macro come from applying those percentages to your daily calorie number. Each gram of a macronutrient is worth this many calories:
Protein: 4 calories/gram
Carbs: 4 calories/gram
Fats: 9 calories/gram

This can be a little confusing so let's look at an example.
Let's say we have a male that weighs 220 pounds, is 6' 0", and 30 years old. He has a lightly active lifestyle but he's looking to lose a little weight.
Weight: 220lbs ÷ 2.2 = 110 kg
Height: 6' 0" = 72 inches, 72 in x 2.54 = 182.9 cm

BMR: (10 x 110 (kg)) + (6.25 x 182.9 (cm)) – (5 x 30 (y)) + (5 (kcal / day)) x 1.375 (lightly active) = 1,988 calories
Because he wants to lose weight, let's reduce his daily caloric intake by 15%.
To find this: 1,988 x .15 = 298 cals, 1,987 - 298 = 1690 kcal/day
To lose weight he needs these %: 40 carbs/40 protein/20 fats
Carbs = 1690 x .40 = 676 cals, 676/4 = 169 g carbs/day
Proteins = 1690 x .40 = 676 cals, 676/4 = 169 g protein/day
Fats = 1690 x .20 = = 338 cals, 338/9 = 36 g fat/day
